Preparations to file a case today against those involved in human trafficking in the name of visit visa from Tribhuvan Airport

A case has been registered today against those involved in human trafficking in the name of visit visa from Tribhuvan Airport. Based on the police report, District Public Prosecutor's Office Kathmandu is going to register a case against 22 people including 7 immigration officers for offenses related to organized crime. District Public Prosecutor's Office Kathmandu has made final preparations to prosecute the visit visa case.

Although it has been 60 days since the investigation of the case yesterday, the case is going to be registered today because the courts under Patan High Court have been granted mourning leave. The District Police Complex, Kathmandu submitted a report to the District Public Prosecutor's Office Kathmandu on January 14 with an opinion to prosecute 22 people involved in the visit visa case.

Based on the same report, the public prosecutor has filed a case in the Kathmandu District Court and is about to register a case related to organized crime. Apart from that, people who set up visit visas, prepare fake documents and people who try to go abroad after preparing their fake documents are going to be filed as defendants. During the investigation by the police, it has been revealed that various people have formed an organized group and are smuggling people in the name of visit visa.
